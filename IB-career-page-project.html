Interstate Batteries Career Page Redo

I spent my summer 2018 working at Interstate Batteries Coporate Office as a front end web developer intern. During Green Carpet, Interstate Batteries' onboarding and orientation, us interns were introduced to our summer long group project. At the end of our 10-week internship, we were to develop initiatives for how Interstate Batteries can target millennials to raise awareness for their brand and status as an employer of choice.

Millennials have become an extremely vital group for companies to learn how to reach. Companies must capture interest of this generation because by the year 2020, millennials will make up between 46-50% of the workforce and begin spending approximately 1.4 trillion annually. Our task was to suggest ways Interstate Batteries could increase its visibility among millennials. On the final day of our internship, we would then present our suggested solutions in a 10-minute presentation to an audience consisting of company Executives, Intern Buddies, Intern Managers, and other team members.

Task

Interstate Batteries' summer '18 interns were tasked with looking at Interstate’s culture, marketing strategies, and recruiting tactics and use what you find to develop a plan for attracting millennials to Interstate. In order to complete this project, we had to identify the characteristics and interests of millennials, as well as best practices for marketing to and recruiting them. We then identified aspects of Interstate Batteries that would draw in millennials.

Ultimately, groups had to develop a marketing and recruiting initiative that leverages IB’s culture, programs, and initiatives to gain the interest of millennials. To develop a solution through the lens of Interstate's culture, we had to familiarize ourselves with team members from different departments, the company culture, and current marketing campaigns and recruiting initiatives. In an effort to bring different backgrounds, skills, personalities, and ideas together, our groups consisted of four interns from different departments.

Planning

As the web developer intern, I realized my ability to produce something tangible such as a web page on IB's website that could be used to encourage millennials to apply. I knew from personal experience that a company career page is a popular target for job applicants and individuals seeking to know more about the business.

Being involved in the internship program, we realized the quality of life, culture, and atmosphere at IB deserved to be publicized and could truly make an impact on IB's brand. We reasoned that adding this content to the company website provides a key source of information for future potential recruits, especially for us mobile-based millennials.

Looking at the existing career page on IB's website, we determined that we'd logically rearrange content to emphasize calls-to-action, remove unnecessary content, and briefly introduce and link to newly created pages.

Analysis

To generate ideas for new content to add to the revamped career page, I audited career pages belonging to other well-established companies with a large portion of millennial workers such as Southwest, Airbnb, Epsilon, Pinterest, etc. and crafted a list of common components and identified their draw and pull factors. After determining categories of content to place on the new career page, I took a deep dive into all things Interstate Batteries.

I started by contacting and setting up meetings with various departmental heads who could enlighten me on the mechanics, culture, events, perks, and benefits of IB. From there, I went online and explored a gold mine of information on Interstate's online intranet portal, IB Connect. I even skimmed the employee handbook. Ultimately, I was able to discover a plethora of quality content worth exposing in an effort to attract millennial applicants.

Design & Implementation

When designing each new page using Adobe XD and Photoshop, I utilized IB's style guides, media library, theme documentation, and built-in web components in order to align my pages with the overarching website theme. Though IB's CMS, Sitecore, had page building functionalities, I opted to code each page by hand using Visual Studio Code, Dreamweaver, and Bootstrap 3 then later translate my work to Sitecore.

Going page by page, I determined; page layouts, what researched content and media to include, what calls-to-action to emphasize, and the amount interactivity necessary per page. For example, on the main career page, I decided to move the 'Apply Now' div towards the top of the viewport to direct attention to the apply button early on. I also included the same apply now call-to-action on every newly created page.

Career Landing Page

For the career landing page, I wanted to create a space that highlights company perks, programs, and features. After auditing various career pages around the web, I decided to create new web pages highlighting Interstate's company culture, internship program, benefits, and military hiring initiative.

My first plan of attack when designing the new career page was to move the "Apply Now" button towards to top of the viewport in order to draw more attention to it. Also, given that the landing page menu links to those additional career related pages, I chose to offer a brief preview of the content found on each newly created page using pre-existing page building components found within Sitecore in order to match my page style with the overarching website style. Lastly, I decided to include a rotating carousel of quotes from current company employees as a way to make Interstate's culture more transparent to potential candidates in a word-of-mouth style.

Life at Interstate

On the Life At Interstate web page, I really wanted to emphasize the empowering, team based company culture while also giving a holistic view of departments and business units found at Interstate. I reckoned that the page content depicting Life @ Interstate would be a worthy pull-factor in and of itself and would generate job applicants.

Starting at the top, I implemented an immediate call to action by telling viewers to "Join Our Interstate Team". Right below the CTA, I gave a concise yet to the point overview of business units found at Interstate, so that potential applicants can gauge just which team they'd like to join.

The next section of the page detailed Interstate's commitment to fostering a culture when your most impactful self can shine. As an intern, I was highly impressed with the company offered courses, programs, and other career enhancing resources and was eager to show the world a sneak peek.

The last section on the page highlighted just how "fun" working at Interstate could be. I wanted to introduce applicants to the exciting recurring company-wide events.

Internships

The internship page was definitely my favorite page to work on. With the other pages, I utilized a lot of company resources in order to generate page content. With the Internship page, I used my everyday experiences, and feedback from my intern peers as well as former interns to tell a tale.

I started off by giving an overview of the internship program offered at Interstate Batteries, including types of internships offered. As I applied for an internship with Interstate, this very material was nowhere to be found and I essentially went in blindly with my fingers crossed (turned out great, though!). Having gone through the internship program, I knew that most everything I participated in was high quality and worth a mention.

I wanted to incorporate something similar to the testimonials on the career landing page as a way to tell potential intern applicants about the program from the point of view of recent participants. My group and I created an internship survey that we sent it out to the other summer '18 interns asking them a few questions about their experience as an IB intern. After receiving responses, I made a design choice to emphasize stand-out quotes from the survey replies we received and opted to hide the rest behind a pop up modal to prevent page congestion. We also sent out a similar survey to current Interstate employee's who had previously interned with the company as a way to show applicants that there's a real chance at a future at Interstate.
