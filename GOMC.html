Google Online Marketing Challenge

The Google Online Marketing Challenge(GOMC) is a global competition where students select a business or nonprofit organization and deploy Google AdWords and/or Google+ advertisement campaigns with a $250 budget/grant. Throughout the challenge, participting students gain hands-on experience creating and executing online marketing campaigns.

Students grow their knowledge of AdWords and SEM and are eligible to earn the AdWords Fundamentals Achievement Certificate after passing the Academy for Ads AdWords Fundamentals Assessment. While GOMC is now retired, Google is set to phase out a new edition: Google Ad Grants Online Marketing Challenge, for non-profits only with an increased AdWords Grant of $10,000.

Cause, Client, & Campaign

My Web Analytics groupmates and I selected The Storehouse of Collin County as our GOMC campaign candidate. The Storehouse is a non-profit organization that consists of three initiatives. The first and largest, Seven Loaves Food Pantry, provides groceries, hygiene products, and other supplies for Collin County individuals and families that quality for assistance under the TEXCAP guidelines.

Similarly, Joseph's Coat, their second initiative, provides gently used clothing through a voucher system with Seven Loaves Food Pantry. Finally, the Project Hope initiative provides a mentorship, education, and training program for women 18 or older who slip through the cracks of social agencies to help them become self-sufficient and improve their lives and the lives of their families.

For the purpose of our project, we focused our campaign on Seven Loaves and Joseph's Coats initiatives and their efforts to provide for people in need.
